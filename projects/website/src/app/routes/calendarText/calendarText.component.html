The Sacred Tzolkin Calendar of the Maya


The sacred Tzolkin calendar has been passed down from generation to generation since the date of creation. Studying astronomy and astrology played a central role in the ancient Maya culture. It helped them understand the past, lead their lives in the present, and foresee the future. Observatories were built to analyze the stars and track the movements of the planets, sun, moon and galaxies, and they were able to perfectly predict eclipses 3,500 years into the future.

The Tzolkin calendar includes 13 months of 20 days each, totaling 260 days for one year. The word “tzolkin” literally means to count the days, which was very important to the Mayas and this calendar was used to keep track of ceremonial and religious events.

Each person in the Maya communities took their societal role based on the astrological energy sign of the day and year they were born, one out of 20 so-called "nawals" or guardian spirits. Each nawal is associated with an animal or element of nature important to the Maya as well as associated character traits, qualities and tendencies. This system kept peace and harmony amongst the Mayas for thousands of years.
